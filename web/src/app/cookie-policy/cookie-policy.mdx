# Cookie Policy

_Last updated: February 2026_

This Cookie Policy explains how Sigil ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website and use our Protocol. By using Sigil, you agree to the use of cookies as outlined in this policy.

## What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help the site recognize your device and store information about your preferences or past actions.

## Types of Cookies We Use

- **Essential Cookies**
  Necessary for the Protocol to function properly. These enable core features such as wallet connection, session management, and security. You cannot opt out of essential cookies as they are required for basic functionality.

- **Functionality Cookies**
  Allow the Protocol to remember choices you make (such as your preferred verification channels or display preferences) and provide enhanced features.

- **Analytics Cookies**
  Help us understand how visitors interact with Sigil by collecting and reporting information anonymously. We use this data to improve the Protocol and user experience.

## Wallet Connection and Web3 Data

When you connect your wallet to Sigil:
- We store session tokens to maintain your authenticated state
- Wallet address information may be cached locally for performance
- No private keys or seed phrases are ever stored or transmitted

## Third-Party Cookies

We integrate with third-party services that may set their own cookies:
- **Privy** - For wallet authentication and session management
- **Analytics providers** - For usage statistics (anonymized)

These services operate under their own privacy policies.

## Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through settings. You can usually configure your browser to:

- Accept or reject all cookies
- Notify you when a cookie is set
- Delete cookies manually or automatically

Please note that disabling essential cookies may prevent you from using certain features of the Protocol, including wallet connection and verification.

## Local Storage and Web3

In addition to cookies, Sigil may use browser local storage to:
- Cache verification status for faster loading
- Store user preferences
- Maintain connection state with blockchain networks
